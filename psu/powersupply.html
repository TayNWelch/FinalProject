What is it?

The power supply unit (PSU) does exactly what its name says. It gives power to all components in your computer.

Popular brands and their prices

There are tons of different brands to choose from when it comes to a PSU. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Seasonic $80-$600
  • Corsair $60-$600
  • Super Flower $90-$450
  • Be Quiet $90-$210
  • EVGA $60-$450
  • NZXT $95-$370

Other things to consider

There are many things to consider when choosing a power supply. The first thing is whether you want a modular PSU or non-modular. With a modular power supply, you only plug in the cables you need. When it comes to non-modular, all the cables are permanently attached. If you don't use a cable, it cannot be removed and must be tucked in the back of the case which can make it difficult when you cable manage. Always use the cords that came with the PSU. NEVER use cords that came with a different PSU! You can fry a whole case of components by using the wrong cords. The other thing to consider is how much wattage you will need to power your PC. If you buy a super high-end GPU and CPU and buy a low wattage PSU, your computer will not run.
